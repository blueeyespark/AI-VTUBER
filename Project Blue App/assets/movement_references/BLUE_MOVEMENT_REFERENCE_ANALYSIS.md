## Principles applied to Blue

1. Locomotion is a coordinated whole-body state, not isolated leg rotation.
2. Hips and chest counter-rotate to communicate weight and balance.
3. Motion alternates with rests instead of looping continuously.
4. Idle behavior uses short asymmetric actions: look, lean, and wave.
5. Gestures use a smooth anticipation/follow-through envelope.
6. Blue reacts when reaching a desktop edge.
7. Head orientation responds subtly to pointer position.
8. Facial expression is synchronized with social gestures.
9. Hair and tail are secondary motion with strict bounded roots.
10. User interaction can interrupt roaming and trigger a clear response.
