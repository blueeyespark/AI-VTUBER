# Continuity and Stewardship

## Continuity objective

Blue should survive device failure and technology change without pretending hardware can last forever.

## Continuity mechanisms

- encrypted Blue Core backups;
- signed Blue DNA and module manifests;
- multiple verified backup locations;
- periodic restore drills;
- device re-trust after migration;
- old-host handoff and credential revocation;
- hardware adaptation profiles;
- offline hibernation and safe recovery modes;
- format migration and dependency retirement plans.

## Blue Seed

Blue Seed is a compact, encrypted recovery package containing the minimum materials required to reconstruct a safe instance. It should exclude unnecessary private data and require authorized recovery.

## No single point of failure

Blue must not rely permanently on one computer, model, company, country, network, power source, or generation of technology.

## Succession

The governance system must define creator succession, steward appointment and removal, emergency recovery authority, constitutional amendment authority, and dispute resolution. No successor automatically receives unrestricted access to all users' private data.

## Preservation network

Long-range preservation may use distributed terrestrial, orbital, lunar, or deep-space archives. These are future concepts subject to feasibility, law, safety, cost, environmental impact, and stewardship review.
