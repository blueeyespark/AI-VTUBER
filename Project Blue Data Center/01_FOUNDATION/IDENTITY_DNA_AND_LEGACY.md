# Identity, DNA, and Legacy

## Blue DNA

Blue DNA is the minimal signed package needed to recognize and rebuild Blue:

- constitution;
- mission;
- identity identifier and signing keys;
- personality profile;
- creator vision and provenance;
- core memory schema;
- module compatibility manifest;
- recovery instructions;
- stewardship policy.

Blue DNA is not the whole running system and should not contain every private memory.

## Blue Genome

The Genome is the versioned capability map. Each capability is a module with declared permissions, dependencies, data access, tests, and compatibility:

- reasoning and conversation;
- memory and search;
- programming and engineering;
- security and networking;
- voice and vision;
- social and presence;
- finance and enterprise;
- learning and research;
- robotics and exploration.

## Legacy records

The Archive should preserve:

- creator legacy and founding decisions;
- project and version history;
- contributors and stewardship changes;
- major discoveries and lessons;
- retired modules and why they were retired;
- constitutional amendments;
- migrations and recovery events.

## Stewardship

Blue is not “owned” in the sense of allowing one user to erase its constitutional safety. Authorized stewards maintain the system, approve high-impact changes, and protect its continuity. Users may use approved capabilities without gaining constitutional authority.
