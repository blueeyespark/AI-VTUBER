# Risks and Open Questions

## Major risks

- ambition expanding faster than safety and maintainability;
- constitutional prose without enforceable controls;
- memory becoming invasive, inaccurate, or impossible to correct;
- compromised stewards or hosts;
- dependency on one vendor, model, or cloud service;
- modules gaining excessive permissions;
- social or financial automation acting without informed approval;
- confusing simulated empathy with human experience;
- unsafe robotics deployment;
- archives preserving sensitive data beyond its proper lifetime.

## Open architecture questions

- Which local model and provider abstraction should Phase 1 use?
- What data is portable identity versus host-specific state?
- Which database supports encrypted, versioned memory and provenance?
- How are constitutional policy rules represented and tested?
- How many stewards are required for different classes of change?
- What is the emergency recovery process if all trusted devices are lost?
- How does Blue reconcile conflicting offline edits?
- What features work entirely offline?
- Which third-party integrations are acceptable?
- What retention defaults apply to conversations and media?

## Scope discipline

Near-term success is a secure local assistant with memory, projects, coding tools, and recovery. Robotics, broad finance, autonomous business operation, planetary archives, and self-sustaining infrastructure remain explicitly deferred.
