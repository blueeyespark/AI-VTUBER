## Safe expansion foundations

The v3.2 expansion database provides audited local records for:

1. PC action proposals and permission profiles
2. Trusted-host candidates, synchronization plans, and revocation plans
3. Mobile invitation, notification, and approval-flow plans
4. Community drafts, moderation cases, rules, and events
5. Enterprise teams, calendar records, inventory, and operations
6. Finance budgets, account records, reconciliation notes, and treasury policy
7. Medical educational notes, source summaries, care questions, and emergency plans
8. Robotics simulations, telemetry samples, safety cases, and emergency-stop plans
9. Peaceful exploration simulations, science goals, environment models, and risks
10. Seed manifests, migration plans, succession records, and hibernation plans
11. World-model entities, events, relations, and provenance-tagged claims
12. Research questions, source candidates, claim reviews, and contradictions

Every expansion record has a domain, type, provenance, risk, safety boundary,
approval requirement, and tamper-evident audit event.

## Non-bypassable boundaries

- Expansion records never enable external execution.
- Finance cannot transact, trade, transfer, or execute advice.
- Medical records cannot diagnose or prescribe treatment.
- Robotics and exploration cannot command hardware.
- Community records cannot publish or send.
- Network/mobile records cannot connect or create remote-control channels.
- Automation records cannot click, type, launch, install, or change the PC.
- Secret-looking values are rejected.
- World-model claims are not treated as verified truth.
- Autonomous self-modification remains blocked.

## Remaining planned integrations

- Twitch/OBS authenticated control and moderation
- Malware scanning/remediation through an installed security provider

Those integrations require separate credentials, provider-specific tests,
revocation, approval, and incident-recovery work.
